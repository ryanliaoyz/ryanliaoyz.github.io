1. **From Expectations to Change of Numeraire**
   > A walk through how risk-neutral pricing generalizes when you change the reference asset, and why this reframing simplifies otherwise gnarly derivative pricing problems.

2. **Recursion Ordering Is Design Intent**
   > Pre-order, post-order, and in-between are statements about where the work lives — not traversal trivia. Mismatched ordering forces workarounds (member variables, reference params, return tuples).

3. **Binary Search Is a Family, Not an Algorithm**
   > Leftmost, rightmost, find-exact, search-on-answer. Each variant has a precise predicate and convergence rule. Tweaking conditions empirically is the bug source; naming the pattern is the fix.

4. **The Cumulative Difference Trick: `exactly = atMost(k) − atMost(k−1)`**
   > When a quantity is hard to compute directly but its cumulative form is easy, take the difference. Generalizes to sliding windows ("exactly k distinct"), prefix sums ("subarrays summing to exactly K"), and beyond.
